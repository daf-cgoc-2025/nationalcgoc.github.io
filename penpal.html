The first thing to know

Your mentor is not your sponsor.

When you get your first assignment, your gaining unit assigns a sponsor to handle your PCS logistics. Your mentor is here for the long game: leadership, networking, and officer development. Two different jobs.

How it works

Four steps to a mentor in your corner.

  1. 01

    Sign up

    Tell us who you are and what you're looking for. Two minutes, right from this page.

  2. 02

    Get matched

    We match you with a CGO (or a cadet) by career field, goals, and preference.

  3. 03

    Connect

    Meet virtually at least once every 4–6 weeks, through commissioning and your first months on station.

  4. 04

    Grow

    Build a network across the force before you ever pin on your gold bars.

What a cadet gets

The unvarnished truth, from someone who just lived it.

Straight talk, no filter

What active duty is actually like, from officers who hit the same challenges a few years ago. The stuff nobody writes down, shared straight, before you need it.

A network before your gold bars

Connections across the Air & Space Force, not just your first base.

The "unwritten rules"

The stuff you're not taught at ROTC, OTS, or the Academy, from working with Senior NCOs to reading squadron dynamics.

A safe space for "dumb" questions

A sounding board for what you'd hesitate to ask your first commander or flight chief.

Career navigation, day one

An early read on assignment processes, deployment tempo, and setting yourself up in your AFSC/SFSC. Plus CGOs who want to welcome you into the community from the moment you arrive.

Why it matters

No new Lieutenant should show up to their first assignment not knowing a single face.

The commitment

It works both ways.

One virtual check-in every 4–6 weeks. A call, video chat, or a detailed email, through commissioning and your first months on station.

Mentees drive it

  • Come with specific questions, goals, or scenarios.
  • Respect your mentor's time and operational schedule.

Mentors give it straight

  • Honest, candid feedback, and they share failures as readily as wins.
  • Show up for the cadence you signed up for.

"The relationship is what both parties are willing to make of it."

Questions

Good to know.

Who's eligible?
Senior cadets and newly commissioned officers sign up as mentees; active-duty CGOs sign up as mentors. AFROTC and USAFA cadre can refer cadets directly through the sign-up form.
How much time does it take?
One virtual check-in, a call, video chat, or a detailed email, at least once every 4–6 weeks. The relationship is what you make of it.
Is this a sponsorship program?
No. Your gaining unit assigns a sponsor for the logistics of your PCS. Your mentor is for your development, networking, and career. See the comparison near the top of this page.
Who can refer cadets?
ROTC detachment and Academy cadre can submit cadets directly through the sign-up form. Just choose "Mentee" and enter the cadet's details.
What happens to my information?
It's used only to match you within the DAF CGOC Mentorship Program. (This is a preview build. Final privacy language will be confirmed before launch.)
